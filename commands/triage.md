Pre-filter the idea in `$ARGUMENTS` so you don't spend a full viability-research run (hours, plus real cost) on something already killed or obviously dead on arrival. **Cost cap ≈ a few dollars** — this is a filter, not a verdict. Triage can KILL or ESCALATE; it can never greenlight — only your full viability-proofing process greenlights.

Steps:

1. **Check the graveyard first — don't re-derive settled kills.** Read `<YOUR_KILL_LOG>` (a running file where you record previously-killed ideas and why) plus any standing "verdict" memories. If this idea is one of them, or a re-skin of one, the verdict is **KILL (settled)** — cite the prior verdict and stop.

2. **Apply your known veto patterns.** Maintain your own list here as you learn what reliably kills ideas for you. Examples of the kind of pattern worth tracking:
   - **Distribution constraint** — if your growth channel is unproven, who reaches the first 100 users, concretely? "SEO + social" alone is a common fail.
   - **Commoditized wedge** — is the core feature already free from an incumbent?
   - **Legal/eligibility walls** — licensing, account-type rules, or ownership requirements that block the model outright.
   - **Proximity** — does it need insider access or expertise you don't have and can't cheaply get?

3. **One cheap reality check** — a single subagent, ~10 minutes of web search: who already owns this space, is the wedge already free, any regulatory wall. Distilled findings only, no report.

4. **Verdict**, a few lines each:
   - **KILL** — matches a settled kill or a hard veto; name it. Append one line recording the kill (idea, date, reason) to your kill log so it stays settled.
   - **ESCALATE** — survives triage; recommend the full viability-proofing process and hand over the triage findings as its starting context.
   - **NEED-INFO** — one specific question that determines which way it goes.

Keep it brutal and fast. The point is protecting the expensive pipeline, not being fair to the idea.

`<YOUR_KILL_LOG>` — point this at wherever you keep a running list of killed ideas, e.g. a markdown file at your projects root.
